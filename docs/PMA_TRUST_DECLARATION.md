# AXIOM PROTOCOL PRIVATE MEMBERSHIP ASSOCIATION TRUST
## Declaration of Trust and Organization

---

**EFFECTIVE DATE:** December 11, 2025

**TRUST NAME:** Axiom Protocol Private Membership Association Trust

**TRUST TYPE:** Private Irrevocable Unincorporated Association Trust

---

## ARTICLE I: DECLARATION OF TRUST

### 1.1 Formation and Purpose

We, the undersigned Trustees, hereby declare that we hold and will hold in Trust all property, rights, and interests now or hereafter transferred to this Trust, for the benefit of the Members of the Axiom Protocol Private Membership Association Trust (hereinafter "the Trust" or "Axiom PMA Trust"), in accordance with the terms and conditions set forth herein.

This Trust is established as a Private Membership Association operating under the protections afforded by the United States Constitution, including but not limited to:
- **First Amendment**: Freedom of association and assembly
- **Fourth Amendment**: Right to privacy and security
- **Fifth Amendment**: Due process protections
- **Ninth Amendment**: Retention of unenumerated rights
- **Tenth Amendment**: Powers reserved to the people
- **Fourteenth Amendment**: Equal protection and due process

### 1.2 Private Nature

This Trust and its associated Private Membership Association operate entirely within the private domain. All activities conducted herein are private contractual matters between consenting Members who have voluntarily agreed to the terms of membership. This Trust does not engage in public commerce and all transactions occur within the private membership structure.

### 1.3 Governing Law

This Trust shall be governed by the common law and the constitutional protections of the United States of America, with recognition of natural law principles and the inherent rights of individuals to freely associate and contract.

## ARTICLE II: DEFINITIONS

### 2.1 Key Terms

**"Association"** means the Axiom Protocol Private Membership Association Trust and all of its Members, Trustees, Protectors, and Officers.

**"Beneficiary"** means any Member entitled to receive benefits from Trust assets or operations.

**"Blockchain"** means the distributed ledger technology utilized by the Trust for record-keeping, governance, and token operations.

**"Contribution"** means any asset, currency, cryptocurrency, intellectual property, or service provided to the Trust by a Member.

**"Member"** means any individual or entity that has been duly admitted to the Association through completion of the Membership Agreement.

**"Membership Token"** means the AXM token or any other digital token issued by the Trust to evidence membership rights and benefits.

**"Protector"** means an individual or entity appointed to oversee Trustee actions and protect Member interests.

**"Trustee"** means an individual or entity appointed to manage Trust affairs in accordance with this Declaration.

## ARTICLE III: TRUST STRUCTURE AND GOVERNANCE

### 3.1 Hierarchy of Authority

The Trust shall operate under the following hierarchical structure:

1. **Grantor(s)**: Original creators and settlers of the Trust
2. **Board of Trustees**: Fiduciary managers of Trust affairs
3. **Protector Council**: Oversight body ensuring Trustee compliance
4. **General Members**: Beneficiaries and participants in Trust activities

### 3.2 Board of Trustees

#### 3.2.1 Composition
The Board of Trustees shall consist of no fewer than three (3) and no more than nine (9) Trustees, appointed according to the procedures outlined in the Bylaws.

#### 3.2.2 Powers and Duties
The Trustees shall have the following powers and duties:
- Manage all Trust assets in the best interest of Members
- Execute contracts on behalf of the Trust
- Oversee blockchain operations and smart contract deployment
- Approve new Member applications
- Distribute benefits to Members according to Trust terms
- Maintain accurate records of all Trust activities
- Ensure compliance with this Declaration and the Bylaws

#### 3.2.3 Fiduciary Responsibility
All Trustees owe a fiduciary duty to the Members and shall act in good faith, with prudence, and solely in the interest of the beneficiaries.

### 3.3 Protector Council

#### 3.3.1 Purpose
The Protector Council serves as an independent oversight body to:
- Monitor Trustee actions for compliance with Trust terms
- Intervene in cases of Trustee breach or misconduct
- Approve extraordinary actions as specified in the Bylaws
- Represent Member interests in disputes

#### 3.3.2 Powers
The Protector Council may:
- Remove and replace Trustees by majority vote
- Veto specific Trustee actions as defined in the Bylaws
- Call emergency Member meetings
- Initiate amendments to Trust documents

### 3.4 On-Chain Governance Integration

#### 3.4.1 DAO Overlay
The Trust incorporates a Decentralized Autonomous Organization (DAO) structure for operational governance. Members may participate in governance through:
- Proposal submission
- Voting on operational matters
- Delegation of voting rights
- Participation in committees

#### 3.4.2 Scope of DAO Authority
The DAO shall have authority over operational matters as defined in the Bylaws. However, the Board of Trustees retains ultimate legal authority and fiduciary responsibility. DAO decisions that conflict with Trustee fiduciary duties or applicable law shall be subject to Trustee override.

#### 3.4.3 Emergency Override
In cases of emergency threatening Trust assets or Member safety, Trustees may take immediate action without prior DAO approval, provided they report to Members within seventy-two (72) hours.

## ARTICLE IV: MEMBERSHIP

### 4.1 Eligibility

Membership in the Axiom Protocol PMA Trust is open to any natural person or legal entity that:
- Is at least eighteen (18) years of age (for natural persons)
- Agrees to the Membership Agreement and all Trust documents
- Completes the membership application process
- Pays required membership dues or contributions
- Is not prohibited by law from participating

### 4.2 Membership Rights

Upon admission, Members shall have the following rights:
- Access to Trust services and platforms
- Participation in governance activities
- Receipt of membership tokens (AXM)
- Pro-rata share of distributions as determined by the Trustees
- Voting rights proportional to token holdings
- Access to Member communications and meetings
- Right to propose amendments and initiatives

### 4.3 Membership Obligations

Members agree to:
- Abide by this Declaration, Bylaws, and Code of Conduct
- Maintain the private nature of Association activities
- Pay membership dues and contributions as required
- Not engage in public commerce using Trust resources
- Indemnify the Trust against claims arising from Member misconduct
- Participate in dispute resolution procedures

### 4.4 Termination of Membership

Membership may be terminated:
- Voluntarily by Member upon written notice
- For cause by Trustee action following due process
- Upon death of natural person Member
- Upon dissolution of entity Member
- For non-payment of dues after notice period

### 4.5 Membership Tokens

#### 4.5.1 Nature of Tokens
Membership tokens (AXM) evidence membership rights and benefits. Tokens are:
- Non-transferable except to other Members
- Subject to compliance restrictions
- Revocable upon termination of membership
- Evidence of membership, not ownership of the Trust itself

#### 4.5.2 Token Rights
Token holders may:
- Vote on governance matters (one token, one vote)
- Receive distributions as approved by Trustees
- Access token-gated services and benefits
- Delegate voting rights to other Members

## ARTICLE V: TRUST ASSETS AND OPERATIONS

### 5.1 Trust Assets

Trust assets include, but are not limited to:
- All cryptocurrency and digital assets held by the Trust
- Smart contracts deployed on behalf of the Trust
- Intellectual property owned by the Trust
- Real property acquired for Trust purposes
- Contributions received from Members
- Revenues generated from Trust operations

### 5.2 Blockchain Operations

The Trust operates blockchain infrastructure including:
- AXM Token ecosystem on Arbitrum One
- Smart contract suite for governance and operations
- DePIN (Decentralized Physical Infrastructure) nodes
- KeyGrow tokenized real estate platform
- SUSU rotating savings programs
- Lumina social media platform

### 5.3 Distributions

#### 5.3.1 Authority
Distributions to Members shall be authorized by the Board of Trustees following procedures outlined in the Bylaws.

#### 5.3.2 Types of Distributions
Distributions may include:
- Patronage refunds based on participation
- Member benefits and rewards
- Governance token allocations
- Access to services and platforms

### 5.4 Prohibited Activities

The Trust shall not:
- Engage in public commerce or solicitation to non-Members
- Operate as a public company or issue securities to the public
- Guarantee returns or profits to Members
- Engage in activities prohibited by applicable law

---

## ARTICLE VI: AMENDMENTS

### 6.1 Amendment Process

This Declaration may be amended by:
- Two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Board of Trustees, AND
- Approval of the Protector Council, AND
- Ratification by majority of voting Members

### 6.2 Notice

Proposed amendments must be communicated to all Members at least thirty (30) days before the vote.

### 6.3 Limitations

No amendment shall:
- Eliminate the private nature of the Association
- Remove constitutional protections
- Retroactively affect vested Member rights without consent

---

## ARTICLE VII: DISSOLUTION

### 7.1 Voluntary Dissolution

The Trust may be dissolved by:
- Unanimous vote of Trustees
- Approval of Protector Council
- Two-thirds (2/3) vote of Members

### 7.2 Distribution Upon Dissolution

Upon dissolution, Trust assets shall be distributed:
1. First, to satisfy all debts and obligations
2. Second, to Members pro-rata based on token holdings
3. Third, any remainder to charitable purposes consistent with Trust objectives

---

## ARTICLE VIII: GENERAL PROVISIONS

### 8.1 Severability

If any provision of this Declaration is deemed invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

### 8.2 Entire Agreement

This Declaration, together with the Bylaws and Membership Agreement, constitutes the entire agreement governing the Trust and supersedes all prior agreements.

### 8.3 No Waiver

Failure to enforce any provision shall not constitute a waiver of the right to enforce such provision in the future.

### 8.4 Dispute Resolution

All disputes shall be resolved through the internal dispute resolution procedures outlined in the Bylaws before any external action is taken.
