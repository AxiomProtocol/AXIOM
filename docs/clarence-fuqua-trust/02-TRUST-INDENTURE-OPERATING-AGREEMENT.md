## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Grantor has established the CLARENCE FUQUA BUSINESS TRUST (the "Trust") pursuant to the Declaration of Business Trust dated ___________________; and

WHEREAS, the parties desire to set forth the operational procedures, rights, duties, and responsibilities governing the administration and management of the Trust;

NOW, THEREFORE, the undersigned parties agree as follows:

---

## ARTICLE I: DEFINITIONS

### Section 1.1 — Definitions

As used in this Trust Indenture, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below:

**"Business Operations"** means the trucking, freight transportation, logistics, and related commercial activities conducted by the Trust.

**"Declaration"** means the Declaration of Business Trust establishing the Clarence Fuqua Business Trust.

**"Extraordinary Transaction"** means any transaction involving:
- The sale, transfer, or encumbrance of substantially all Trust assets;
- The assumption of debt exceeding $50,000 or such other amount as may be specified;
- The merger or consolidation of the Trust with another entity;
- Any transaction with a related party exceeding $10,000 in value.

**"Grantor"** means the individual identified as such in the Declaration.

**"Trust Protector"** means the individual appointed to exercise oversight powers as defined in the Declaration.

**"Trustee"** means the individual or entity responsible for Trust administration and management.

---

## ARTICLE II: BUSINESS OPERATIONS

### Section 2.1 — Scope of Operations

The Trust shall engage in the following primary business activities:

(a) Over-the-road trucking and freight transportation;

(b) Local and regional delivery services;

(c) Logistics coordination and freight brokerage;

(d) Equipment leasing and rental;

(e) Such other activities as are incidental to the foregoing.

### Section 2.2 — Operating Authority

The Trust shall obtain and maintain all necessary operating authority, including but not limited to:

(a) Motor Carrier Authority from the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA);

(b) USDOT Number registration;

(c) State-specific permits and registrations;

(d) International Registration Plan (IRP) credentials, if applicable;

(e) International Fuel Tax Agreement (IFTA) credentials, if applicable;

(f) Unified Carrier Registration (UCR).

### Section 2.3 — Insurance Requirements

The Trust shall maintain the following minimum insurance coverage:

| Coverage Type | Minimum Limit |
|---------------|---------------|
| Commercial Auto Liability | $1,000,000 per occurrence |
| Motor Truck Cargo | $100,000 per occurrence |
| General Liability | $1,000,000 per occurrence |
| Workers Compensation | As required by law |

The Trustee may obtain additional coverage as prudent for business operations.

### Section 2.4 — Contracts and Agreements

The Trustee is authorized to enter into contracts on behalf of the Trust, including:

(a) Freight transportation agreements with shippers and brokers;

(b) Equipment lease and finance agreements;

(c) Service and maintenance agreements;

(d) Employment and independent contractor agreements;

(e) Vendor and supplier agreements.

All contracts shall be executed in the name of the Trust.

---

## ARTICLE III: FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT

### Section 3.1 — Bank Accounts

The Trustee shall open and maintain one or more bank accounts in the name of the Trust for the purpose of:

(a) Receiving payments for services rendered;

(b) Disbursing payments for operating expenses;

(c) Maintaining operating reserves;

(d) Making distributions to beneficiaries.

### Section 3.2 — Revenue Collection

All revenue generated by Trust operations shall be deposited into Trust accounts. The Trustee shall maintain records of all revenue sources.

### Section 3.3 — Operating Expenses

The Trustee is authorized to pay the following categories of operating expenses from Trust funds:

(a) Fuel and maintenance costs;

(b) Insurance premiums;

(c) License and permit fees;

(d) Equipment lease or loan payments;

(e) Professional service fees (legal, accounting, etc.);

(f) Taxes and regulatory assessments;

(g) Employee or contractor compensation;

(h) Communication and technology expenses;

(i) Office and administrative expenses;

(j) Other ordinary and necessary business expenses.

### Section 3.4 — Reserve Requirements

The Trustee shall maintain an operating reserve equal to at least two (2) months of average operating expenses, unless circumstances require temporary deviation from this requirement.

### Section 3.5 — Distributions

After payment of operating expenses and maintenance of required reserves, the Trustee may make distributions to beneficiaries as follows:

(a) Monthly or quarterly distributions as cash flow permits;

(b) Special distributions as approved by the Grantor;

(c) Year-end distributions after accounting for annual expenses.

All distributions shall be documented and reported to beneficiaries.

---

## ARTICLE IV: TRUSTEE DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

### Section 4.1 — Fiduciary Duties

The Trustee owes fiduciary duties to the Trust and its beneficiaries, including:

(a) **Duty of Loyalty** — To act in the best interests of the Trust and beneficiaries, avoiding conflicts of interest;

(b) **Duty of Care** — To exercise reasonable care, skill, and diligence in Trust administration;

(c) **Duty of Prudence** — To make decisions with the care that a prudent person would exercise;

(d) **Duty to Account** — To maintain accurate records and provide accountings as required.

### Section 4.2 — Administrative Duties

The Trustee shall:

(a) Maintain all required licenses, permits, and registrations;

(b) Ensure compliance with applicable laws and regulations;

(c) Maintain insurance coverage as specified herein;

(d) File all required tax returns and pay taxes when due;

(e) Maintain accurate financial records and accounts;

(f) Provide periodic reports to the Grantor and Trust Protector;

(g) Preserve and protect Trust property;

(h) Respond to legal process and regulatory inquiries.

### Section 4.3 — Limitations on Authority

The Trustee shall not, without prior approval of the Trust Protector:

(a) Engage in any Extraordinary Transaction as defined herein;

(b) Admit new beneficiaries to the Trust;

(c) Amend the Declaration or this Trust Indenture;

(d) Terminate the Trust;

(e) Delegate fiduciary responsibilities to third parties.

### Section 4.4 — Conflicts of Interest

If the Trustee has a personal interest in any transaction involving the Trust, the Trustee shall:

(a) Disclose the conflict to the Grantor and Trust Protector in writing;

(b) Obtain approval before proceeding with the transaction;

(c) Document the terms of the transaction and the basis for approval.

---

## ARTICLE V: TRUST PROTECTOR DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

### Section 5.1 — Oversight Role

The Trust Protector serves as an independent overseer of Trust administration, with the authority to:

(a) Review Trustee performance and compliance;

(b) Approve or disapprove Extraordinary Transactions;

(c) Resolve disputes between the Trustee and beneficiaries;

(d) Remove and replace the Trustee for cause.

### Section 5.2 — Approval Requirements

The following actions require Trust Protector approval:

(a) Extraordinary Transactions as defined in Section 1.1;

(b) Material amendments to Trust documents;

(c) Changes to beneficiary designations;

(d) Termination of the Trust;

(e) Assumption of indebtedness exceeding established thresholds.

### Section 5.3 — Removal of Trustee

The Trust Protector may remove the Trustee for cause, including:

(a) Breach of fiduciary duty;

(b) Gross negligence or willful misconduct;

(c) Incapacity or inability to perform duties;

(d) Conviction of a felony or crime of moral turpitude;

(e) Persistent failure to perform administrative duties.

Upon removal, the Trust Protector shall appoint a successor Trustee.

### Section 5.4 — Non-Interference

The Trust Protector shall not direct or interfere with day-to-day business operations or routine administrative decisions.

---

## ARTICLE VI: RECORDS AND REPORTING

### Section 6.1 — Required Records

The Trustee shall maintain the following records:

(a) Original Trust documents and amendments;

(b) Financial statements and bank records;

(c) Contracts and agreements;

(d) Insurance policies;

(e) Licenses, permits, and regulatory filings;

(f) Tax returns and related documents;

(g) Minutes of meetings and resolutions;

(h) Correspondence regarding Trust matters.

### Section 6.2 — Reporting Requirements

The Trustee shall provide the following reports:

| Report Type | Frequency | Recipients |
|-------------|-----------|------------|
| Financial Statement | Quarterly | Grantor, Trust Protector |
| Operating Summary | Monthly | Grantor |
| Annual Accounting | Annually | Grantor, Trust Protector, Beneficiaries |
| Tax Information | Annually | Beneficiaries (as needed) |

### Section 6.3 — Access to Records

The Grantor, Trust Protector, and beneficiaries shall have reasonable access to Trust records upon written request.

---

## ARTICLE VII: LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

### Section 7.1 — Limitation of Liability

The Trustee shall not be personally liable for:

(a) Losses arising from the good faith exercise of discretion;

(b) Losses resulting from circumstances beyond the Trustee's control;

(c) Actions taken in reliance on professional advice.

### Section 7.2 — Indemnification

The Trust shall indemnify the Trustee and Trust Protector against claims, liabilities, and expenses arising from their good faith performance of duties, except for:

(a) Gross negligence or willful misconduct;

(b) Breach of fiduciary duty;

(c) Self-dealing or fraud.

### Section 7.3 — Insurance

The Trustee may obtain fiduciary liability insurance or errors and omissions coverage at Trust expense.

---

## ARTICLE VIII: SUCCESSION

### Section 8.1 — Successor Trustee

If the Trustee is unable or unwilling to continue serving, a successor shall be appointed as follows:

(a) The Grantor may appoint a successor Trustee during the Grantor's lifetime;

(b) If the Grantor is unable to act, the Trust Protector shall appoint a successor;

(c) The successor Trustee shall execute an Acceptance of Trusteeship before assuming duties.

### Section 8.2 — Successor Trust Protector

If the Trust Protector is unable or unwilling to continue serving:

(a) The Grantor may appoint a successor Trust Protector;

(b) If the Grantor is unable to act, the successor shall be appointed by agreement of the Trustee and beneficiaries.

### Section 8.3 — Transition

Upon succession, the outgoing fiduciary shall:

(a) Transfer all Trust records and property to the successor;

(b) Provide a full accounting through the date of transition;

(c) Execute documents necessary to effect the transfer.

---

## ARTICLE IX: AMENDMENT AND TERMINATION

### Section 9.1 — Amendment

This Trust Indenture may be amended by written instrument signed by:

(a) The Grantor and Trustee, with Trust Protector approval for material amendments;

(b) The Trustee and Trust Protector, if the Grantor is deceased or incapacitated.

### Section 9.2 — Termination Events

The Trust shall terminate upon:

(a) Written agreement of the Grantor, Trustee, and Trust Protector;

(b) Distribution of all Trust assets;

(c) Merger or consolidation with another entity, with appropriate approvals;

(d) Judicial order.

### Section 9.3 — Winding Up

Upon termination, the Trustee shall:

(a) Cease business operations in an orderly manner;

(b) Collect outstanding receivables;

(c) Pay all debts, obligations, and expenses;

(d) Distribute remaining assets to beneficiaries;

(e) File final tax returns and regulatory notices;

(f) Maintain records for the required retention period.

All notices under this Trust Indenture shall be in writing and delivered to the addresses set forth in the Declaration.

### Section 10.2 — Governing Law

This Trust Indenture shall be governed by the laws of the State of _____________________.

Disputes arising under this Trust Indenture shall be resolved as follows:

(a) First, by good faith negotiation among the parties;

(b) If unresolved, by mediation before a mutually agreed mediator;

(c) If still unresolved, by binding arbitration in accordance with the rules of the American Arbitration Association.
