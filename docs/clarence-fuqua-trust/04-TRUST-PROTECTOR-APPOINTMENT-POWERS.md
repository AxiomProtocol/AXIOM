## PART II: POWERS OF THE TRUST PROTECTOR

### Section 1 — General Powers

The Trust Protector shall have the following powers, to be exercised in a fiduciary capacity for the benefit of the Trust and its beneficiaries:

### Section 2 — Oversight and Approval Powers

**A. Approval of Extraordinary Transactions**

The Trust Protector must approve in writing before the Trustee may:

(a) Sell, transfer, or encumber substantially all Trust assets;

(b) Assume indebtedness exceeding Fifty Thousand Dollars ($50,000), or such other amount as may be established by amendment;

(c) Enter into any merger, consolidation, or reorganization;

(d) Enter into transactions with related parties exceeding Ten Thousand Dollars ($10,000) in value;

(e) Make capital expenditures exceeding Twenty-Five Thousand Dollars ($25,000);

(f) Enter into contracts with terms exceeding three (3) years.

**B. Approval of Document Amendments**

The Trust Protector must approve in writing any material amendment to:

(a) The Declaration of Business Trust;

(b) The Trust Indenture and Operating Agreement;

(c) Beneficiary designations or beneficial interest allocations.

### Section 3 — Removal and Replacement Powers

**A. Removal of Trustee**

The Trust Protector may remove the Trustee for cause, including:

(a) Breach of fiduciary duty;

(b) Gross negligence in the performance of duties;

(c) Willful misconduct or fraud;

(d) Incapacity or inability to perform duties;

(e) Conviction of a felony or crime involving dishonesty;

(f) Persistent failure to provide required accountings or reports;

(g) Unauthorized self-dealing or conflicts of interest.

**B. Appointment of Successor Trustee**

Upon removal of the Trustee, the Trust Protector shall appoint a successor Trustee within sixty (60) days.

**C. Procedure for Removal**

Before removing the Trustee, the Trust Protector shall:

(a) Provide written notice to the Trustee specifying the grounds for removal;

(b) Allow the Trustee fifteen (15) days to respond or cure the deficiency, if curable;

(c) Consider the Trustee's response before making a final determination;

(d) Document the removal decision in writing.

### Section 4 — Dispute Resolution Powers

**A. Mediation**

The Trust Protector may mediate disputes between:

(a) The Trustee and beneficiaries;

(b) Among beneficiaries regarding Trust matters.

**B. Binding Determination**

If mediation is unsuccessful, the Trust Protector may make a binding determination on disputes regarding:

(a) Interpretation of Trust documents;

(b) Allocation of expenses among beneficiaries;

(c) Timing and manner of distributions.

### Section 5 — Administrative Powers

**A. Amendment of Administrative Provisions**

The Trust Protector may amend administrative provisions of the Trust documents to:

(a) Respond to changes in law or regulation;

(b) Correct ambiguities or errors;

(c) Improve administrative efficiency;

provided that such amendments do not materially affect the beneficial interests in the Trust.

**B. Change of Situs**

The Trust Protector may change the situs (governing jurisdiction) of the Trust if:

(a) Such change would be beneficial for tax or legal purposes;

(b) The change is consistent with the purposes of the Trust;

(c) The Trustee is given reasonable notice.

### Section 6 — Information Rights

**A. Access to Records**

The Trust Protector shall have access to all Trust records, including:

(a) Financial statements and bank records;

(b) Contracts and agreements;

(c) Tax returns and filings;

(d) Insurance policies;

(e) Correspondence regarding Trust matters.

**B. Reports from Trustee**

The Trustee shall provide the Trust Protector with:

(a) Quarterly financial statements;

(b) Prompt notice of any material event affecting the Trust;

(c) Annual accountings;

(d) Any other information reasonably requested.

## PART III: LIMITATIONS ON TRUST PROTECTOR

### Section 1 — Non-Fiduciary Status for Certain Actions

The Trust Protector shall act in a fiduciary capacity, but shall not be liable for:

(a) Good faith exercise of discretionary powers;

(b) Failure to act if no request for action has been received;

(c) Actions taken in reliance on professional advice.

### Section 2 — Prohibited Actions

The Trust Protector shall not:

(a) Participate in day-to-day business operations or management decisions;

(b) Direct specific investment decisions;

(c) Remove the Trustee without cause;

(d) Modify the Trust in a way that would benefit the Trust Protector personally;

(e) Exercise any power for the Trust Protector's own benefit.

### Section 3 — No Duty to Monitor

The Trust Protector is not required to actively monitor Trust operations, but may investigate when:

(a) A concern is brought to the Trust Protector's attention;

(b) The Trust Protector becomes aware of potential problems;

(c) Required reports are not received from the Trustee.
