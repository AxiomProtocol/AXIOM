## ARTICLE III: PURPOSE OF THE TRUST

### Section 3.1 — Business Purpose

The Trust is established for the following lawful business purposes:

(a) To engage in trucking, freight transportation, logistics, and related commercial activities;

(b) To own, lease, operate, and maintain commercial vehicles and transportation equipment;

(c) To enter into contracts for the transportation of goods and materials;

(d) To hold and manage business relationships, including customer contracts, vendor agreements, and service arrangements;

(e) To maintain insurance policies, permits, licenses, and regulatory authorizations necessary for lawful business operations;

(f) To open and maintain bank accounts, receive payments, and disburse funds in furtherance of Trust operations;

(g) To acquire, hold, manage, and dispose of real and personal property necessary for business operations;

(h) To employ or engage independent contractors, agents, and service providers;

(i) To engage in any other lawful business activity consistent with the purposes stated herein.

### Section 3.2 — Limitations

The Trust shall not engage in any activity that is unlawful under applicable federal, state, or local law. The Trust is not organized for charitable, religious, or nonprofit purposes.

## ARTICLE V: POWERS OF THE TRUSTEE

### Section 5.1 — General Powers

The Trustee shall have all powers necessary or appropriate to carry out the purposes of the Trust, including but not limited to:

(a) To conduct business operations and enter into contracts on behalf of the Trust;

(b) To open, maintain, and close bank accounts and other financial accounts;

(c) To receive, deposit, and disburse funds;

(d) To acquire, hold, maintain, insure, and dispose of real and personal property;

(e) To employ professionals including attorneys, accountants, and consultants;

(f) To obtain and maintain insurance coverage;

(g) To obtain necessary licenses, permits, and regulatory authorizations;

(h) To execute and deliver documents, instruments, and agreements on behalf of the Trust;

(i) To delegate administrative functions to agents or service providers;

(j) To invest Trust funds in accordance with prudent investment standards;

(k) To borrow funds and grant security interests in Trust property when appropriate for business purposes;

(l) To initiate or defend legal actions on behalf of the Trust;

(m) To perform all other acts necessary for the proper administration of the Trust.

### Section 5.2 — Standard of Care

The Trustee shall administer the Trust with the care, skill, and diligence that a prudent person would exercise in managing similar business affairs.

### Section 5.3 — Compensation

The Trustee shall be entitled to reasonable compensation for services rendered to the Trust, as determined by the Grantor or as set forth in a separate compensation agreement.

---

## ARTICLE VI: POWERS OF THE TRUST PROTECTOR

### Section 6.1 — Appointment

The Trust Protector is appointed by the Grantor to exercise oversight and specific powers as defined herein.

### Section 6.2 — Powers

The Trust Protector shall have the following powers:

(a) To remove and replace the Trustee for cause, including breach of fiduciary duty, incapacity, or failure to perform duties;

(b) To approve or disapprove certain extraordinary transactions as specified in the Trust Indenture;

(c) To resolve disputes between the Trustee and beneficiaries;

(d) To amend administrative provisions of the Trust to respond to changes in law or circumstances;

(e) To approve amendments to the Trust proposed by the Trustee or Grantor.

### Section 6.3 — Limitation

The Trust Protector shall not have the power to direct the Trustee with respect to day-to-day business operations or investment decisions.

### Section 6.4 — Compensation

The Trust Protector shall be entitled to reasonable compensation for services rendered, as determined by agreement with the Grantor.

## ARTICLE VIII: ADMINISTRATION

### Section 8.1 — Records

The Trustee shall maintain complete and accurate records of all Trust transactions, including financial statements, contracts, and correspondence.

The Trustee shall provide periodic accountings to the Grantor and beneficiaries at least annually, or more frequently upon reasonable request.

### Section 8.3 — Tax Matters

## ARTICLE IX: AMENDMENTS AND TERMINATION

### Section 9.1 — Amendments

This Declaration may be amended by written instrument signed by the Grantor and the Trustee, with the approval of the Trust Protector for material amendments.

### Section 9.2 — Termination

The Trust may be terminated:

(a) By written agreement of the Grantor, Trustee, and Trust Protector;

(b) Upon the occurrence of events specified in the Trust Indenture;

(c) By operation of law.

### Section 9.3 — Distribution Upon Termination

Upon termination, the Trustee shall wind up Trust affairs, pay all debts and obligations, and distribute remaining assets to the beneficiaries as directed.
