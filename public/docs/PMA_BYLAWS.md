# AXIOM PROTOCOL PRIVATE MEMBERSHIP ASSOCIATION TRUST
## Bylaws

---

**EFFECTIVE DATE:** December 11, 2025

**VERSION:** 1.0

---

## ARTICLE I: NAME AND LOCATION

### Section 1.1 Name
The name of this organization shall be the Axiom Protocol Private Membership Association Trust (hereinafter "the Association" or "Axiom PMA").

### Section 1.2 Principal Office
The principal office of the Association shall be located at such place as designated by the Board of Trustees.

### Section 1.3 Digital Presence
The Association maintains its primary operations through blockchain-based infrastructure on the Arbitrum One network and associated platforms.

---

## ARTICLE II: PURPOSE AND OBJECTIVES

### Section 2.1 Primary Purpose
The Association exists to:
- Provide a private framework for Members to participate in blockchain-based financial services
- Develop and maintain decentralized infrastructure for smart city operations
- Enable Member participation in tokenized real estate and DePIN ecosystems
- Facilitate community savings and investment programs
- Support Member education and economic empowerment

### Section 2.2 Specific Objectives
The Association shall pursue the following objectives:
1. Operate the AXM token ecosystem for Member governance and benefits
2. Maintain the KeyGrow rent-to-own platform for Member housing opportunities
3. Operate SUSU savings circles for community-based financial cooperation
4. Deploy and manage DePIN infrastructure nodes
5. Develop the Lumina social platform for Member content creation
6. Provide educational resources through The Forge Academy
7. Facilitate Member governance through DAO mechanisms

---

## ARTICLE III: MEMBERSHIP

### Section 3.1 Classes of Membership

#### 3.1.1 Founding Members
Individuals or entities that join during the founding period (first 12 months) with enhanced governance rights and benefits.

#### 3.1.2 Standard Members
Regular Members admitted after the founding period with full participation rights.

#### 3.1.3 Associate Members
Limited membership for entities providing services to the Association.

### Section 3.2 Application Process

#### 3.2.1 Required Steps
1. Complete the Membership Application form
2. Read and acknowledge the Declaration of Trust
3. Sign the Membership Agreement
4. Complete identity verification (KYC) as required
5. Pay initial membership contribution
6. Receive Membership Token allocation

#### 3.2.2 Review Period
Applications shall be reviewed within fourteen (14) business days.

#### 3.2.3 Approval Authority
The Membership Committee or designated Trustee shall approve or deny applications.

### Section 3.3 Membership Dues and Contributions

#### 3.3.1 Initial Contribution
New Members shall make an initial contribution as established by the Trustees:
- Founding Member: Minimum 1,000 AXM equivalent
- Standard Member: Minimum 100 AXM equivalent
- Associate Member: As negotiated

#### 3.3.2 Annual Dues
Annual dues may be established by Trustee resolution and shall be communicated to Members at least sixty (60) days before the due date.

#### 3.3.3 Special Assessments
Special assessments may be levied by two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Trustees with thirty (30) days notice.

### Section 3.4 Member Rights and Privileges

Members in good standing shall have the right to:
1. Access all Association platforms and services
2. Vote on governance proposals
3. Receive Membership Token allocations
4. Participate in distributions and rewards
5. Propose amendments and initiatives
6. Attend and participate in Member meetings
7. Access Member-only communications
8. Utilize dispute resolution procedures
9. Transfer membership (subject to restrictions)

### Section 3.5 Member Responsibilities

Members agree to:
1. Maintain confidentiality of Association affairs
2. Conduct themselves in accordance with the Code of Conduct
3. Pay dues and assessments when due
4. Participate in governance in good faith
5. Not represent the Association without authorization
6. Not engage in activities harmful to the Association
7. Comply with all Trust documents

### Section 3.6 Discipline and Termination

#### 3.6.1 Grounds for Discipline
- Violation of Trust documents or Code of Conduct
- Conduct detrimental to the Association
- Fraud or misrepresentation
- Non-payment of dues after notice

#### 3.6.2 Disciplinary Procedures
1. Written notice of allegations
2. Opportunity to respond within fourteen (14) days
3. Hearing before Disciplinary Committee (if requested)
4. Decision by Trustees within thirty (30) days
5. Right to appeal to Protector Council

#### 3.6.3 Penalties
- Warning
- Suspension of privileges
- Probation
- Termination of membership

## ARTICLE IV: GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE

### Section 4.1 Board of Trustees

#### 4.1.1 Composition
The Board shall consist of five (5) Trustees:
- Three (3) elected by Members
- Two (2) appointed by founding Trustees

#### 4.1.2 Term of Office
- Elected Trustees: Three (3) year terms, staggered
- Appointed Trustees: Serve at pleasure of appointing authority

#### 4.1.3 Qualifications
Trustees must be:
- Members in good standing for at least one (1) year
- At least twenty-one (21) years of age
- Not convicted of fraud or financial crimes
- Able to pass background verification

#### 4.1.4 Meetings
- Regular meetings: Monthly
- Special meetings: Called by Chair or two (2) Trustees
- Quorum: Majority of Trustees

#### 4.1.5 Voting
- Each Trustee has one vote
- Simple majority for ordinary matters
- Two-thirds (2/3) for extraordinary matters (as defined)

### Section 4.2 Officers

#### 4.2.1 Required Officers
- Chair of the Board
- Secretary
- Treasurer
- Chief Operations Officer

#### 4.2.2 Appointment
Officers shall be appointed by the Board of Trustees.

#### 4.2.3 Duties
**Chair**: Preside at meetings, provide leadership, represent Association

**Secretary**: Maintain records, provide notices, certify documents

**Treasurer**: Oversee finances, provide reports, maintain accounts

**Chief Operations Officer**: Manage daily operations, implement policies

### Section 4.3 Protector Council

#### 4.3.1 Composition
Three (3) Protectors:
- One (1) appointed by founding Trustees
- One (1) elected by Members
- One (1) independent professional

#### 4.3.2 Powers
- Remove Trustees for cause
- Approve amendments to Declaration
- Veto extraordinary transactions (as defined)
- Call emergency Member meetings

### Section 4.4 Committees

#### 4.4.1 Standing Committees
- Membership Committee
- Finance Committee
- Technology Committee
- Governance Committee
- Disciplinary Committee

#### 4.4.2 Special Committees
May be established by Trustee resolution as needed.

### Section 4.5 DAO Governance

#### 4.5.1 Scope
The DAO shall govern:
- Allocation of community funds
- Protocol parameter changes
- Service feature requests
- Community initiatives

#### 4.5.2 Proposal Process
1. Proposal submission (minimum 1,000 AXM to submit)
2. Discussion period: Seven (7) days
3. Voting period: Seven (7) days
4. Implementation (if approved)

#### 4.5.3 Voting Thresholds
- Simple proposals: 50% + 1 of votes cast
- Treasury proposals: 66% approval
- Constitutional proposals: 75% approval

#### 4.5.4 Quorum
Minimum 10% of total voting power must participate.

## ARTICLE V: MEETINGS

### Section 5.1 Member Meetings

#### 5.1.1 Annual Meeting
An annual meeting of Members shall be held within ninety (90) days after the fiscal year end.

#### 5.1.2 Special Meetings
Special meetings may be called by:
- Board of Trustees
- Protector Council
- Petition of 10% of Members

#### 5.1.3 Notice
Written notice shall be provided at least fourteen (14) days before any Member meeting.

#### 5.1.4 Quorum
Twenty percent (20%) of Members represented in person or by proxy.

#### 5.1.5 Virtual Attendance
Members may attend and vote through electronic means.

### Section 5.2 Voting

#### 5.2.1 One Token, One Vote
Each AXM token held confers one vote on Member matters.

#### 5.2.2 Proxy Voting
Members may delegate votes to other Members.

#### 5.2.3 Snapshot Voting
Vote weight determined by token balance at snapshot block.

---

## ARTICLE VI: FINANCIAL PROVISIONS

### Section 6.1 Fiscal Year
The fiscal year shall be January 1 through December 31.

### Section 6.2 Treasury Management

#### 6.2.1 Treasury Wallet
Multi-signature wallet requiring 3 of 5 Trustee signatures.

#### 6.2.2 Spending Limits
- Under $10,000: Officer approval
- $10,000 - $100,000: Two Trustee approval
- Over $100,000: Board approval

### Section 6.3 Financial Reports
Quarterly financial reports shall be provided to Members.

### Section 6.4 Distributions

#### 6.4.1 Authorization
Distributions require Board approval by majority vote.

#### 6.4.2 Allocation
Distributions allocated pro-rata based on Membership Token holdings at record date.

### Section 6.5 Reserves
The Association shall maintain reserves equal to at least six (6) months of operating expenses.

---

## ARTICLE VII: BLOCKCHAIN OPERATIONS

### Section 7.1 Smart Contracts

#### 7.1.1 Deployment Authority
Smart contracts shall be deployed only with:
- Technology Committee review
- Security audit completion
- Board approval

#### 7.1.2 Upgrades
Contract upgrades require:
- DAO governance approval (for non-emergency)
- Or Trustee emergency action with subsequent ratification

### Section 7.2 Token Operations

#### 7.2.1 Minting Authority
New tokens may be minted only as authorized by:
- Tokenomics schedule in Declaration
- DAO governance approval
- Trustee action for operational needs

#### 7.2.2 Burning
Tokens may be burned through:
- Voluntary Member surrender
- Disciplinary action
- Protocol mechanisms (fees, etc.)

### Section 7.3 Security

#### 7.3.1 Key Management
Private keys shall be managed through:
- Hardware security modules
- Multi-party computation
- Geographic distribution

#### 7.3.2 Incident Response
Security incidents shall be addressed per the Security Policy.

---

## ARTICLE VIII: DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### Section 8.1 Internal Resolution

#### 8.1.1 Negotiation
Members shall first attempt good-faith negotiation.

#### 8.1.2 Mediation
If negotiation fails, disputes shall be submitted to mediation before a neutral Member.

#### 8.1.3 Arbitration
Unresolved disputes shall be submitted to binding arbitration under rules established by the Board.

### Section 8.2 Limitations

#### 8.2.1 No Class Actions
Members waive the right to participate in class actions against the Association.

#### 8.2.2 Limitation Period
Claims must be brought within one (1) year of the dispute arising.

---

## ARTICLE IX: AMENDMENTS

### Section 9.1 Procedure

#### 9.1.1 Proposal
Amendments may be proposed by:
- Board of Trustees
- Petition of 10% of Members
- Protector Council

#### 9.1.2 Notice
Proposed amendments shall be distributed to Members at least thirty (30) days before vote.

#### 9.1.3 Approval
Amendments require:
- Two-thirds (2/3) vote of Board
- Majority vote of Members
- Protector Council ratification (for material changes)

---

## ARTICLE X: INDEMNIFICATION

### Section 10.1 Scope
The Association shall indemnify Trustees, Officers, and authorized agents for actions taken in good faith on behalf of the Association.

### Section 10.2 Limitations
Indemnification shall not apply to:
- Criminal conduct
- Gross negligence or willful misconduct
- Self-dealing or conflicts of interest

---

## ARTICLE XI: MISCELLANEOUS

### Section 11.1 Interpretation
These Bylaws shall be interpreted consistent with the Declaration of Trust.

### Section 11.2 Conflict
In case of conflict between these Bylaws and the Declaration, the Declaration shall control.

### Section 11.3 Electronic Communications
All notices and communications may be made electronically.

### Section 11.4 Effective Date
These Bylaws are effective upon adoption by the founding Trustees.
