A Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) and a Special Purpose Entity (SPE) are essentially two names for the same thing: a separate legal entity created for a narrow, specific, or temporary objective.
Often, a company (the sponsor or parent company) creates this entity to isolate financial risk, hold specific assets, or facilitate a complex transaction, such as securitization or project financing.

 * Separate Legal Entity: An SPV/SPE is a distinct company, trust, or limited partnership, meaning its legal existence is separate from its sponsor.
 * Limited Purpose: It is created to fulfill a specific, predefined role (e.g., to finance a single real estate project, to hold a pool of mortgages for securitization, or to make one specific investment).
 * Risk Isolation (Bankruptcy Remote): This is the primary function. The SPV/SPE's assets and liabilities are legally separated ("ring-fenced") from the parent company. If the parent company faces bankruptcy, the SPV's assets are typically protected, and conversely, if the SPV defaults, the parent company's core operations are shielded from the SPV's liabilities.
 * Off-Balance Sheet: In some cases, the SPV's assets and liabilities may not appear on the sponsor's consolidated balance sheet, which can affect the sponsor's financial ratios (though modern accounting rules have tightened the requirements for this treatment).

SPVs/SPEs are powerful tools in finance and are used across various sectors:
 * Securitization: This is one of the most common uses. A parent company (like a bank) transfers assets (e.g., mortgage loans, car loans) to an SPV. The SPV then issues securities (like mortgage-backed securities) to investors, backed by the cash flows from those assets. This is how the risk is distributed to a wider set of investors.
 * Project Financing: Used for large infrastructure or real estate projects to isolate the project's financial risks from the main sponsor's balance sheet.
 * Risk Sharing and Joint Ventures: Allows two or more companies to collaborate on a specific project, with the SPV acting as the subsidiary to share the risks and rewards of that single venture.
 * Tax Efficiency: They may be established in jurisdictions with favorable tax laws for certain types of transactions.
In summary, the terms SPV and SPE are generally interchangeable, describing a legal structure specifically designed to compartmentalize financial activities and manage risk.
